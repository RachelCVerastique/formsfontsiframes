  • My Pets

    • Where we are found

    The Phiddipus audax, also known as the bold jumping spider
    are native to North America and have been introduced to Hawaii,
    Nicobar Islands, Azores and the netherlands.

    What we eat

    Jumping spiders are hunters and do not use a web to catch prey.
    The bold jumping spider eats various small to medium
    insects such as mealworms, flies and crickets.

    Eight legged kittens

    As their common name states, the bold jumping spider is just that BOLD or audacious.
    A french arachnologist first described them as "very bold, often jumping on the hand which threatens it."
    Bites from bold jumpers are rare but can occur if they feel threatened or mishandled
    Though they may look intimidating they really are just eight legged kittens.
